Chapter 1: Tears of a Strephon

I was sitting at my desk going through some documents when that idiot Strephon approached me.

“Uh, sir…” I could tell the trembling Strephon was fixated on my beautiful new hair. “C-can I have the mirror back? It’s… actually not mine and I would like to give it back to its owner.”

“N-now what did I tell you a-a-about stealing things, Strephon? Take the mirror and immediately bring it back to its proper owner!”


I grabbed the strange mirror from my drawer and began handing it over to Strephon.

I stopped. Is that Strephon? I could have sworn that I looked just like Strephon in the reflection of the mirror. Now that I think about it, my hair makes me look a lot like him. I had no idea.

“Uh, sir… Is there something wrong?”

“You know what Strephon? There is. T-there’s something wrong… with me.”

Strephon looked puzzled. But, then again, he always looked puzzled.


I realize the truth now.


“Look, Strephon. H-have I been hard on you? Have I?”

Strephon hesitated for a moment or two and then nodded profusely.

“Ah, I see. Strephon. I’m genuinely sorry. Please understand that the reason I am so hard on you… The reason I…” I stopped for a moment to compose the proper words.

“Strephon, I see myself in you. I really do. When I was your age, I was just like you. And just like you, I was a common target for many of my teachers. But you know what? That made me stronger. Whenever I had a bad day at school, I would run as fast as I could. I wouldn’t be as good at running as I am now if it weren’t for all the trouble I had in my life. In a way, it was resistance training. Strephon… I want you to be strong. I want you to be so strong that you can possess the strength that others may lack. Because one day… One day you may find yourself in a hopeless situation where you’ll have to defend someone you love. And when that day comes… I want you to keep your head up high and your heart even higher. I want you to be the best Strephon you can be.”

Strephon looked at me in the eyes for the first time. He was crying, as usual. His tears, however, were for an entirely different reason.


I went up to Strephon, gave him my mop hair, and patted him on the back. Instead of wiping his eyes, he kept looking at me. Eventually, he gave the mop back to me. He knew I needed it more than he did. He embraced me. We have both been through a lot, but we have both become stronger as well.
